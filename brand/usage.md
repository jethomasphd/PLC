# Brand Usage Guidelines

## Visual vibe
- **Blue-collar dignity**
- **Warmth without sentimentality**
- **Masculine without machismo**
- **Local without cliquishness**
- **Serious without being heavy**

Think: lantern light, work jacket patch, clean signage, civic bulletin board.

---

## Color usage
Primary backgrounds should be the dark inks/navies.
Primary text should be cream/white.
Gold is for emphasis, dividers, buttons, and the “porch light” glow moments.

Avoid bright tech blues, neon gradients, or “startup purple”.

---

## Logo placement
Best placements:
- top-left nav (small badge)
- hero section (medium badge)
- footer (medium)
- membership card / RSVP confirmation

Never:
- stretched wide across a header
- tiled as a background
- used as a watermark behind text

Clear space:
- minimum padding = height of the lantern flame (approx rule of thumb)

---

## Photography
If using photos:
- candid, natural light, real dads
- hands, coffee, strollers, backyards, garages, grills, parks
- avoid “stock boardroom smiles” and glossy influencer vibes

Use a dark overlay behind text (60–80% ink).

---

## Iconography
Use simple, sturdy icons:
- lantern, star, shield, handshake, home, compass, calendar
No thin-line “app icon set” look. Prefer filled or heavier strokes.

---

## UI feel
- big readable typography
- low cognitive load
- short copy blocks
- strong hierarchy
- a couple of strong CTAs, not five

Motion:
- minimal, purposeful
- slow fades, no bouncy “SaaS” animations
