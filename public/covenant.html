The Covenant

What this is. What this isn't. How to post.

This is

  • A porch light: steady, local, calm
  • A bulletin board for fathers helping fathers
  • Structured messages: wins, struggles, asks, offers
  • Real talk, practical takeaways
  • Anonymous by design — no profiles, no accounts
  • Offline-first: the real work happens in person

This is not

  • A feed
  • Social media
  • A debate arena
  • Therapy
  • Politics
  • Performance
Posting Rules
  • One message at a time. Pick a type: Win, Struggle, Ask, Offer, or Reading.
  • 500 characters max. Say what matters. Leave the rest.
  • No threads. Flat list, most recent first. No replies, no threads, no pile-ons.
  • No likes. No reactions. Read it or don't. No engagement score.
  • Proof-of-work required. Your browser solves a small puzzle before posting. This keeps the pace calm.
Before You Post

You'll check three boxes:

  • "Not politics. Not therapy. Not a feed."
  • "Be brief. Be real. Be useful."
  • "Assume goodwill."

If your message doesn't fit these, save it for the meetup.

Message Types
  • WIN — One win. Something that went right this week.
  • STRUGGLE — One pressure point. No rants. Just the weight.
  • ASK — One question. Something you need help with.
  • OFFER — One offer. Something you can share or do.
  • READING — One quote and why it matters. Bring it to the next meetup.
How to Post

You need the council passphrase.

The passphrase is shared in person at meetups. It rotates regularly.

This isn't a password. It's proof you showed up.

No account. No email. No sign-up. Just come to a meetup, get the phrase, and post when you have something worth sharing.

Why It's Built This Way

Anti-Flood by construction.

Most platforms are built to maximize engagement. This one is built to minimize noise.

  • No infinite scroll — just the last 25 messages
  • No algorithms — most recent only
  • No metrics — no likes, no views, no trending
  • No notifications — come back when you want to
  • Proof-of-work — takes a few seconds to post, by design

"The porch light stays on. It doesn't flash."

After You Post

You'll receive a Lantern Receipt: a unique hash ID, your message type, and a timestamp.

This is your proof of posting — without identity. No name attached. Just proof you contributed.
